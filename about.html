Michael Lison

German Engineer | Automation Consultant | Software Developer | Engineering Educator

Michael Lison is a German mechanical engineer with over 20 years of experience leading industrial automation and engineering design projects across multinational corporations in Germany, Taiwan, and Greater China. His career spans the full spectrum of industrial engineering — from factory automation and production line optimization to mechanical design, software development, and technical education.

After completing his mechanical engineering studies in Germany, Michael held senior engineering positions at leading industrial companies where he spearheaded factory modernization programs, implemented Industry 4.0 initiatives, and developed cross-disciplinary engineering standards. His work has directly contributed to significant efficiency gains, energy reductions, and quality improvements across automotive, semiconductor, and machinery sectors.

In 2024, Michael founded DerMech Solution to bridge German engineering rigor with the dynamic manufacturing landscape of Asia. His vision: deliver end-to-end engineering consulting that combines CE/DIN standards compliance with practical, implementation-ready solutions tailored to each client's unique challenges.

Beyond consulting, Michael is passionate about engineering education. He has mentored numerous students in STEM programs, coached national competition teams in Germany, and advocates for project-based learning that connects theoretical knowledge with real-world engineering practice.

EDUCATION & MENTORING

Engineering the Next Generation

Michael believes that engineering excellence is not just about technical competence — it's about cultivating a mindset of curiosity, rigor, and continuous improvement. Through DerMech Solution's education initiatives, he brings this philosophy to students, professionals, and organizations.

His experience includes coaching national-level engineering competition teams in Germany, designing STEM curricula for secondary and tertiary education, and delivering hands-on workshops in mechanical design, Arduino/Python programming, and project-based engineering. These programs emphasize practical problem-solving over theoretical memorization, preparing participants for real-world engineering challenges.

Whether mentoring a student's first Arduino project or guiding a professional team through a complex automation deployment, the approach remains the same: German engineering discipline, applied with precision and purpose.
