Semiconductor Cleanroom Automation

Wafer Handling System Modernization

Challenge

A leading semiconductor manufacturer in Taiwan needed to upgrade their 8-inch wafer handling system. The existing system had reached end-of-life status with increasing maintenance costs and decreasing accuracy. The client required a solution that would: increase throughput by 30%, reduce contamination incidents to near-zero, maintain ISO 14644 Class 5 cleanroom standards, and integrate with their existing manufacturing execution system (MES).

Solution

We designed a comprehensive wafer handling modernization package: Custom robotic gripper system with precision positioning (±0.1mm accuracy), cleanroom-compatible materials (316L stainless steel, PTFE, EPDM seals), real-time contamination monitoring with particle counters, redundant safety interlocks per IEC 61508, and full MES integration via OPC UA protocol. The design incorporated modularity for future 12-inch wafer upgrade capability.

Implementation

The project followed our 8-phase engineering process over 7 months: Phase 1–2: On-site assessment of existing infrastructure and MES interface requirements. Phase 3–4: FEA simulation for gripper mechanism optimization and cleanroom airflow analysis. Phase 5–6: Precision manufacturing of components in our certified workshop, followed by FAT (Factory Acceptance Testing) with simulated wafer loads. Phase 7: Installation during scheduled production downtime, with full commissioning and operator training. Phase 8: 30-day performance monitoring and optimization.

Results

  • Throughput increased by 35% (exceeding 30% target)
  • Contamination incidents reduced by 98%
  • Positioning accuracy: ±0.08mm (better than ±0.1mm spec)
  • MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures) increased from 450 to 2,800 hours
  • Complete ISO 14644 Class 5 compliance verified

Customer Benefits

The client achieved a 28% reduction in total cost of ownership within 18 months, with payback period of 14 months. The modular design allows seamless upgrade to 12-inch wafer processing, protecting their investment. Operator training in German engineering methodology created lasting internal competency. The project earned recognition as a benchmark for cleanroom automation in the Taiwanese semiconductor industry.

Automotive Production Line

Electric Vehicle Battery Module Assembly Line

Challenge

An automotive tier-1 supplier in Germany needed to design and implement a fully automated battery module assembly line for electric vehicle battery packs. Requirements included: 100% traceability per ISO 9001, cycle time under 45 seconds per module, defect rate below 50 PPM (parts per million), and compliance with IEC 61508 safety standards for automated systems.

Solution

We delivered a turnkey automated assembly line comprising: 12-station inline configuration with conveyor system, robotic cell with 6-axis collaborative robots for module assembly, laser welding station with real-time quality monitoring, 3D vision inspection system, automatic screwdriving with torque verification, and a central SCADA system for line monitoring and data logging. Safety architecture included light curtains, emergency stop circuits, and safety PLC per ISO 13849-1 Category 3.

Implementation

The 14-month project was divided into three parallel workstreams: mechanical design and fabrication (Months 1–6), electrical and control system integration (Months 4–9), and software development with FAT (Months 7–12). Installation and commissioning occurred during a 4-week plant shutdown. We provided comprehensive operator training and created detailed maintenance manuals in German, English, and Chinese for the multinational workforce.

Results

  • Cycle time achieved: 42 seconds per module (target was 45 seconds)
  • Defect rate: 23 PPM (target was below 50 PPM)
  • Production capacity increased by 40%
  • Quality audit score: 99.7% first-pass yield
  • Full CE marking documentation delivered for machinery directive compliance

Customer Benefits

The client now operates one of the most advanced EV battery assembly lines in Europe, with capability to scale production by 60% without additional floor space. The comprehensive documentation and training enabled their team to achieve independent operational status within 3 weeks of handover. The project contributed to securing two additional major EV manufacturer contracts worth EUR 45M annually.

Food Processing Hygienic Automation

Automated Sauce Production Line

Challenge

A specialty food manufacturer in Taiwan producing premium sauces needed to automate their manual bottling and labeling process. The client faced challenges with: inconsistent fill volumes (±10% variance), high labor costs (18 operators across 3 shifts), frequent contamination events requiring line shutdowns for cleaning, and inability to meet increasing export order volumes due to capacity constraints.

Solution

We designed a hygienic automated bottling line with 8 stations: volumetric filling system with ±0.5% accuracy, induction sealing, automatic capping, weight verification per batch, label application with OCR verification, case packing, and palletizing. All equipment met FDA 21 CFR and EU 1935/2004 food contact material regulations. The design featured CIP (Clean-in-Place) capability with automated flushing cycles.

Implementation

The 6-month project minimized production disruption by implementing in two phases: Phase 1 (Months 1–3): Parallel installation of automated equipment alongside existing manual lines. Phase 2 (Months 4–6): Gradual transition with operator retraining and process fine-tuning. We conducted HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points) review and established food safety protocols. Installation included sanitary stainless steel (316L) framework, tri-clamp fittings for easy disassembly, and CIP-compatible piping systems.

Results

  • Fill volume accuracy improved to ±0.4% (from ±10%)
  • Labor reduced by 72% (from 18 to 5 operators)
  • Production capacity increased by 85%
  • Contamination incidents reduced to zero
  • Export order fulfillment rate increased from 68% to 99.5%

Customer Benefits

The client achieved payback in 11 months through labor cost savings and reduced waste (contaminated product losses decreased by EUR 380,000 annually). The automated line enabled them to accept new export contracts previously impossible due to capacity constraints, generating EUR 2.1M in additional annual revenue. The hygienic design earned them a new supplier qualification from a major European retail chain.
